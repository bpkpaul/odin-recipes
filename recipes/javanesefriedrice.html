Javanese Fried Rice

Description

Nasi Goreng Jawa, or Javanese fried rice, is a traditional Indonesian dish that stands out from other varieties of fried rice for its bold, smoky flavor and brown color. Unlike Chinese-style fried rice, which is often light and savory, Javanese fried rice uses sweet soy sauce (kecap manis) as its main seasoning, giving the dish a rich caramelized taste. It is typically cooked over a high flame, often with a charcoal stove in traditional settings, which lends the rice its signature smoky aroma.

This dish is often enjoyed as a hearty breakfast or late-night supper, topped with simple accompaniments such as a fried egg, fresh cucumber slices, and prawn crackers. Depending on the region, it may also include shredded chicken, vegetables, or even satay as side dishes. With its balance of sweet, savory, and slightly spicy notes, Javanese fried rice is more than just comfort food, it’s a nostalgic dish that captures the warmth of family gatherings and the bustling atmosphere of Indonesian street food culture.

Ingredients

  • 600 g cooked rice, preferably a day old
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 5 shallots
  • 3 red chilies
  • 2 tbsp sweet soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp shrimp paste (terasi), roasted
  • 2 eggs
  • 150 g shredded chicken (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooking oil, as needed
  • Garnishes: fried shallots, cucumber slices, prawn crackers, sunny-side-up egg

Steps

  1. Prepare the spice paste by grinding garlic, shallots, chilies, and roasted shrimp paste into a smooth mixture.
  2. Heat oil in a wok, then sauté the spice paste until fragrant.
  3. Add the eggs, scramble lightly, then stir in the shredded chicken if using.
  4. Add the cooked rice, breaking up any clumps, and stir-fry over high heat.
  5. Season with sweet soy sauce, regular soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Stir continuously until the rice is evenly coated and slightly smoky.
  6. Remove from heat and serve hot, topped with fried shallots, cucumber slices, prawn crackers, and a fried egg.
